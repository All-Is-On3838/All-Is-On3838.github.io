Problem Introduction

Title

Does presence of testing sites influence testing numbers?

Industry Focus

COVID Testing

Problem Statement

Deep dive into data analytics to show value of increasing free testing sites.

Business Use Case

  1. Does establishment of testing sites increase testing in that community?

Goals/ metrics

  • Find the correlation between presence of neighborhood testing sites and testing numbers in the surrounding counties.
  • Isolate testing data for 4 weeks before a popup site is established and 4 weeks after.

Deliverables

  • A Jupyter notebook outlining findings and recommendations.

Dataset list

  • Outbreak numbers by region from MDHSS.gov
  • New press releases from MDHSS.gov and other news sites to gather popup site dates and locations

Websites to scrape the data

  • Outbreak numbers By Region
  • MDHSS New Press Releases

What I Learned

For this project, cleaning and isolating the data was pretty forward. Where I struggled was with gathering data and formatting my data for visualizing.

A key component of my data comprises dates COVID testing sites are open. There is no public dataset of this data. I had to figure out a way to isolate this on my own. I started by looking at news press releases from MDHSS; this pointed me toward three websites of available testing sites. I was going to use this data to accurately assess testing numbers based on available testing sites, but I couldn't look at any of the data because MDHSS no longer used those sites. From here, I shifted strategies and used the news press releases to approximate when and where sites opened. I quickly realized that the press releases left significant holes in the timeline. I learned there were 22 "neighborhood sites" that opened over the last two years. These were sites that provided no-cost testing, so I thought I could compare no-cost testing to pharmacy testing. This would change my question to investigate whether or not it was worth it for MDHSS to continue funding neighborhood sites. However, I was unable to find significant pharmacy data to compare to neighborhood sites.

At this point I went back to my news press release idea, and expanded my news search to local news stations. This produced promising data. I was able to isolate several counties that opened limited pop up sites. This allowed me to investigate my original question of: Does establishing a COVID Testing Site increase testing?

My other problem was with formatting my data for visualization. In order to create dataframes that matched a single county to multiple counties, I choose to aggregate data. I learned a lot about indexing and eventually ended up using a join statement to rename the indexes, essentially flattening it.

Conclusions and Recommendations

H0 => µ1 = µ2 (population mean of Bay County is equal to Surrounding Counties)
HA => µ1 ≠µ2 (population mean of Bay County is different from Surrounding Counties)
Since the p-value (0.00001) is less than alpha = 0.05 so we reject the null hypothesis of the test. We have sufficient evidence to say that the mean of total test numbers between the two data groups is significantly different.

Bay County has significantly more tests on average than the surrounding counties. However, one must note that this is true before and after pop up sites are set up. Therefore, there is something beside pop up site presence that increases testing numbers.
